Ursula Le Guin

Bright the hawk's flight
On the empty sky.

One of the great novelists of the late 20th Century, Ursula Le Guin died on January 22, 1918, approximately a year ago. Her works of speculative fiction include The Lathe of Heaven (strongly influenced by Taoism), The Dispossessed (an “ambiguous Utopia” heavily influenced by anarchist political theory), the Left Hand of Darkness (a milestone in feminist science fiction), the remarkable Four Ways to Forgiveness (a science-fictional exploration of slavery), as well as the 6-volume Earthsea series, perhaps the most serious work of fantastical fiction in the English language.
